What Is a Legal Heir?
Legal heir refers to a person chosen by the law to inherit properties, assets, and liabilities of a deceased individual. It does not matter whether there was nomination or beneficiary designation in the case. Legal heirship depends on succession law and can never change based on the nomination form signed or the beneficiary designated. The closest family members such as spouse, children, and parents become legal heirs in most cases. When there are no close relations available, other members of the extended family succeed the deceased, again, in a certain sequence. Simply put, the legal heir of an individual is recognized as the one authorized by law to own whatever assets belong to him/her.
What Is a Nominee?
Nominee refers to any person named by a living individual in the case of life insurance payments, deposit accounts in a bank, or any other asset that may be received after the death of the deceased. However, the nominee is only entitled to receive the asset and does not become the legal owner automatically. This point has been reiterated by the Supreme Court of India as well as several high courts across the country. A nominee is simply a facilitator or custodian of an estate until legal heirs present their proof.
Insurance Act, 1938: What Every User Needs to Know
The Indian Insurance Act, 1938, is the principal legislation governing the insurance sector in India. From a website perspective, however, the important provisions are those concerning nomination, particularly section 39. Designating a nominee ensures timely receipt of claims by the policyholder, without affecting the legal heirs' rights according to the succession laws.
Who Is This Site Intended for?
This website is meant to serve members of the Christian and Parsi communities, whose asset succession laws are governed by the Indian Succession Act, 1925, as opposed to religion-based personal laws such as the Hindu Succession Act. If you belong to either group and wish to know how your assets will be distributed among different people upon your demise, please check our website.
